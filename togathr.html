Summary

Industry:

Hospitality and Events

Project Team:

Agile Team of 9 (5 Designers + 4 Developers)

Tasks:

User research, Branding, wireframes, UI design

ToGathr is an AI-powered web platform designed to make event planning effortless. It consolidates budgeting, vendor management, guest tracking, and team collaboration into one intuitive space. Our goal was to reduce the stress of event planning and replace fragmented workflows with a seamless, end-to-end digital solution.

My Role

Design
In the UX design phase, I designed the information architecture for the entire platform followed by the user flow for the overview, budget and collaborators sections. In the UI design phase, I created the brand color palette. Additionally, I worked on the Overview and Collaborators pages. I was also in charge of all the custom illustrations developed for the platform.
Collaboration
I had the opportunity to work as the project manager during the course of the design and development of this project. We worked using the agile methodology with each sprint being 1 week long. I was responsible for facilitating the sprint planning and sprint review meetings and managed the team's workflow on Trello.

Problem

Event planning is fragmented and stressful, often requiring multiple apps to manage budgets, guests, and vendors. Finding trusted vendors is also difficult, with many relying on word-of-mouth or unreliable online reviews.

Research

Primary and secondary research was conducted to understand how users plan events, identify pain points, and uncover opportunities to streamline the process through an all-in-one platform.

Secondary research - Competitor Analysis

It was observed that the online event planning space was either mainly focused on weddings or large scale events involving ticket sales. There were simply not enough platforms catering to the need of personal event planning.

Primary research - User Interviews & Personas

Two groups of users were interviewed.

Interview Insights

Event planning is collaborative People often seek input and help from friends and family, making teamwork an essential part of the process.
Budgeting is central Managing expenses is one of the biggest challenges users face when planning an event.
Getting started is overwhelming Many users feel unsure and intimidated at the beginning of the planning journey, not knowing where to begin.

The Solution

ToGathr is a web platform that uses AI to simplify personal and professional event planning by combining task management, budgeting, vendor search, guest RSVPs, and team collaboration in one place.

Togathr | Key features

Figma Prototype Togathr Web platform

Event Creation

The event creation process is made highly intuitive and engaging for new users

Event Overview

This page gives users a quick glimpse of all the major aspects of their event. Additionally, every user gets a task list based on the event type they selected during the event creation process so they don’t have to start form scratch.

Vendors

Users can browse through hundreds of vendors and find their best match.

Budget tracking

Users can track their budget with ease as most of it is automated. When the user books a vendor the price of that booking is added to the budget.

Collaborators & Guest lists

ToGathr makes event planning smarter and more collaborative by automating budget tracking and enabling teamwork. As users book vendors, costs are automatically added to their budget, giving them real-time insights. They can also invite friends, family, or colleagues to join the planning process and assign tasks, making collaboration seamless and organized.

Branding, Design Systsem and Hand-off

ToGathr’s branding is warm, modern, and approachable, reflecting the joy and connection at the heart of every event. The visual identity uses clean layouts, soft yet vibrant colors, and playful illustrations to make planning feel less like a chore and more like part of the celebration. It's designed to build trust, reduce stress, and invite collaboration, whether the user is organizing a small gathering or a large-scale event.

To ensure smooth front-end implementation, we built a centralized design library where developers can easily reference all UI elements, including components, color styles, and card layouts that are used throughout the platform. And during the hand-off phase, all the screens were annotated to ensure a smooth handover to the development team.

Conclusion

ToGathr addresses real pain points in event planning by centralizing critical tasks into one responsive, user-friendly platform. Through iterative design, deep user research, and thoughtful collaboration between designers and developers, we built a tool that brings structure to chaos.
The most important learning for me through this project was the importance of prioritization. As a team we underestimated the effort it would take to design and develop so many features within a set time period of 12 weeks. I think that a more focused approach with reduced features would have helped us achieve better refinement.
